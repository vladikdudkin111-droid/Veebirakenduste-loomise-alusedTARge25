Breed history

Dog breeds originated thousands of years ago from ancestral wolves, with selective breeding driven by early humans to enhance traits like hunting ability, protection, and companionship. While ancient types like Greyhounds existed by 9,000 BCE, most modern breeds were standardized during the 19th-century Industrial Revolution, shifting from function to aesthetic, uniform standards.
